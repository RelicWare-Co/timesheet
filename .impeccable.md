## Design Context

### Users

Colombian workers (primarily Spanish-speaking) who need to track their work hours, breaks, and overtime to verify they are being paid correctly by their employers. They use the app on mobile phones during breaks or after shifts, often in physically demanding job contexts. The primary job-to-be-done is: "Am I being paid what I'm owed?"

### Brand Personality

**Warm, organized, protective.** Three words: _caring, clear, trustworthy_.

- The interface should feel like a reliable companion that has your back.
- It is not aggressive or confrontational — it empowers through clarity and calm.
- Emotional goal: confidence and peace of mind, not anxiety or anger.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Warm utility with human touch.** High-contrast but softened with warm tints. Rounded corners (8-12px), generous whitespace, and a paper-like cream background. Typography pairs a friendly display face (Bricolage Grotesque) with a clean body face (Onest). The design should feel like a well-designed field notebook crossed with a modern productivity app.

- Theme: Both light and dark modes. Light = warm cream paper. Dark = warm charcoal.
- Anti-references: Cold corporate dashboards, aggressive brutalist UIs, generic SaaS templates, cartoon illustration apps.

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration.** Every visual element must serve information hierarchy. Colors are warm and purposeful — green for success/growth, terracotta for warmth, red only for destructive actions.
2. **Typography creates personality.** Bricolage Grotesque for headings (friendly, human), Onest for body (clear, modern). Size and weight create structure.
3. **Show value immediately.** Users should understand their pay situation within 3 seconds of opening the app. No configuration walls without context.
4. **Protect the user.** Every interaction that destroys or commits data must provide clear, reversible feedback. The app is an advocate, not a recorder.
5. **Mobile-first comfort.** Large touch targets, minimal taps, readable text sizes, and zero hidden gestures. If it works comfortably on a phone during a lunch break, it works everywhere.
